## Lecture 4/6/26

A **random variable (RV)** assigns the outcome of a random experiment to a numerical value.

The **distribution** of a RV is the set of all possible values the RV can take.
* Discrete RVs maps outcomes of a random experiment to a discrete numerical value.
    * An **expected value**, $\mathbb{E[x]} = \sum_{range(x)} xP(x)$, is the probability weighted average of RV x.
* Continuous RVs maps outcomes of a random experiment to a continuous numerical value.
  * Continuous RVs follow a probability density function.
  * Properties:
    * $P(x) = $\int_{-\inf}^{x} f(x)dx
    * $0 \leq P(x) \leq 1$
    * $\int_{-\inf}^{\inf} f(x)dx = 1$
